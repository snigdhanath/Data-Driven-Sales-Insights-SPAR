# SPAR Retail Analytics & Data Governance Project

## Project Overview

As part of my Master's degree in IT Project Management, I collaborated with a team of five members to analyze retail sales and inventory data for SPAR. The project focused on identifying seasonal demand patterns, regional sales performance, data quality issues, and opportunities to improve inventory planning and business decision-making.

Using data analysis, process improvement techniques, and stakeholder-focused recommendations, we developed a framework to support more accurate forecasting, stronger data governance, and more efficient inventory management.

---

## Business Challenge

SPAR required a deeper understanding of:

* Seasonal demand fluctuations across key product categories
* Regional sales performance differences
* Revenue growth and decline trends
* Data quality issues affecting reporting accuracy
* Opportunities to optimize inventory planning and stock allocation

The objective was to transform raw sales data into actionable business insights that could support strategic and operational decision-making.

## Project Approach

### 1. Data Consolidation & Preparation

* Consolidated multiple sales datasets into a unified reporting structure
* Cleaned and transformed raw retail data
* Identified missing values, anomalies, and negative turnover records
* Created an enriched dataset for analysis and reporting

### 2. Seasonal Sales Analysis

Analyzed sales trends for key product categories:

* Wine
* Sweets
* Cosmetics

Key objective:

* Identify recurring seasonal demand patterns
* Support inventory planning during peak periods such as Easter, Carnival, and Halloween

### 3. Geographic Performance Analysis

Evaluated sales performance across different regions and store formats to:

* Identify high-performing regions
* Detect underperforming locations
* Understand regional purchasing behavior
* Support localized inventory strategies

### 4. Revenue Trend Analysis

Performed trend analysis to:

* Measure revenue growth and decline patterns
* Identify periods of reduced performance
* Highlight opportunities for promotional campaigns and pricing strategies

### 5. Data Governance Assessment

Investigated data quality issues including:

* Negative turnover values
* Missing records
* Inconsistent sales entries

Developed governance recommendations to improve reporting reliability and data integrity.

---

## Key Recommendations

### Inventory Optimization

* Implement predictive inventory planning based on seasonal demand patterns
* Increase stock availability before major seasonal events
* Optimize inventory allocation by store segment and region

### Revenue Growth

* Introduce targeted promotions during low-performing periods
* Develop product bundle campaigns to increase cross-category sales
* Improve pricing strategies using historical demand insights

### Data Governance

* Enforce validation rules at Point-of-Sale (POS) systems to prevent negative turnover values from entering operational databases
* Establish data quality monitoring processes and anomaly reporting controls

### Change Management

* Deliver structured training workshops for regional inventory teams
* Support adoption of predictive stocking practices
* Reduce implementation risks and warehouse bottlenecks during process transition
